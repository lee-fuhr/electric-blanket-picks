Electric blankets have come a long way from the scratchy, single-setting throws of the past. Today’s best models offer multiple heat zones, programmable timers, and fabrics that rival your favorite bedding. They can lower your thermostat at night while keeping you toasty, which is a win for both comfort and energy bills. When you’re shopping, focus on three things: control (how many heat settings and timers), fabric (softness and washability), and size (make sure it fits your bed and intended use). Safety certifications and auto shut-off are standard now, but double-check if you’re buying a very old stock or a no-name brand.
